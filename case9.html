CollaBubbles

CollaBubbles: A Multimodal Interface to Support Collocated Collaboration and Ideation

Abstract

Exploration in the aesthetic visualization of speech has, so far, been confined to the realm of animated graphics and perfomance artwork. However, research exploring phonesthesia (phonetic symbolism) suggests that people often perceive speech synaesthetically in a similar manner, deriving associations from other perceptual domains like shape and texture based on the sound of the word. Inspired by Golan Levin and Zachary Lieberman’s “In Situ Speech Visualization” performances, this project plays around with creating a multimodal interface geared for synchronous, collocated collaboration that allows the users to enclose their utterances in speech bubbles within a whiteboard-like interface.

Study Outcome

This project explores the creation of a collocated, collaborative auditory mind map. By creating a multimodal interface that uses elements of touch and voice, we combine the merits of Bolt’s put-that-there paradigm with speech to text transcription to create a visual record of the collaborative discussion that can be played back and understood with the support of a spatio-temporal index. This project is still a work-in-progress, so stay tuned for updates!
